CUPS sizing using the QSR1®
Quantifies the remaining pipe wall thickness

The results from the QSR1 provides the truly quantitative values of the minimum remaining wall thicknesses along the pipe, Twall. The CX sensors measures Twall along the scanned pipe length. This is ideal for sizing corrosion under simple pipe supports because it provides the minimum remaining wall thickness and the corrosion length. Our new AX sensors can measure how the Twall varies around the pipe circumference. This is applicable for sizing corrosion at simple, clamped and saddle supports. In addition, verticle and angled pipes can also be inspected with the AX sensors.



Accurate measurements with Frequency Analysis

The QSR1® uses a frequency-based method which directly relates the frequency properties of the waves to the pipe wall thickness. In contrast to this, conventional NDT techniques, such as MRUT, typically use Amplitude-based methods, which involves estimating defect sizes based on the amplitude of the signals. The advantages includes improved accuracy and lower measurement uncertainties because frequency analysis has less significant effect to signal energy losses to pipe coatings, liquid loading and support pads.
Reliable measurements
with automation

The QSR1® is designed to semi-automatically scan the pipe. This means that the scanning procedure is not hand operated by an inspector, but by motorized wheels, which improves the reliability of data measurements. The calibration procedure is also automatically performed on the pipe specimen, without the use of any calibration blocks. Our user-friendly WaveProQSR software helps our inspectors to easily perform both calibration and scanning.
